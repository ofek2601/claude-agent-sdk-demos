## 3. REGULATORY CHANGES AFFECTING HEALTHCARE FACILITIES

### State-Level REIT Regulatory Changes
- **States Considering Healthcare REIT Legislation 2026+**: 20+ states
- **Trigger Event**: Steward hospital failures in Massachusetts and subsequent bankruptcies
- **Affected Major REITs**: CareTrust REIT, Medical Properties Trust, Omega Healthcare Investors, Sabra Health, Welltower

### Federal Healthcare Policy Changes for 2026
- **Medicare Part D Out-of-Pocket Drug Cost Cap**: $2,100 annually (effective January 1, 2026)
- **Compliance Burden**: Healthcare organizations must comply with 625+ regulatory requirements across 9 domains
- **Non-Clinical Regulatory Spending Estimate**: $39 billion annually by providers
- **HIPAA Compliance Cost Range**: $80,000 - $120,000 (mid-range)
- **Hospital Non-Compliance Rate**: 75%+ of hospitals not in full compliance (July-November 2024)

### 2026 Specific Compliance Changes
- **Hospital Price Transparency Rule Changes**: Finalized to strengthen requirements beginning 2026
- **Site-Neutral Payment Expansion**: CMS expanding site-neutral payment policies
- **Prior Authorization Rules**: Enhanced tightening
- **Medicare Drug Price Negotiations**: First round implementation continuing

### Administrative & Funding Risks
- **Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac Privatization Risk**: Potential changes to major senior housing private market transaction funding source
- **NIH Funding Cuts**: Potential reduction affecting life sciences investment
- **Pharmaceutical Tariff Risk**: Potential tariffs on pharmaceutical companies
- **COVID-Era Policy Expiration**: ACA subsidies and other pandemic policies ending

## 8. KEY RISK FACTORS FOR 2026

### Interest Rate Risk
- **Risk Factor**: Rate-cutting cycle pause due to inflation resurgence
- **Impact**: Valuation expansion ceiling for REITs
- **Mitigation**: Companies with high occupancy and strong rent growth positioned better

### Regulatory Risk
- **State-Level**: 20+ states considering healthcare REIT legislation
- **Federal**: Multiple policy changes and site-neutral payment expansions
- **Compliance**: 75%+ hospital non-compliance rate with existing regulations

### Reimbursement Risk
- **Medicare**: Continued physician fee schedule cuts expected (-2.83% in 2025)
- **Medicaid**: Enrollment declining but some state rate increases offsetting
- **Provider Margin Pressure**: High labor costs and inflation outpacing reimbursement growth

### Capital Cost Risk
- **Debt Pricing**: Unsecured REIT debt at 6.5% average (elevated)
- **Fed Policy**: Potential pause in rate cuts could limit capital market improvements
- **Refinancing**: 6.4-year weighted average debt maturity approaching refinancing needs
